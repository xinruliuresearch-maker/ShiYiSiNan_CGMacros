# Nature Food 150-word Abstract v1.0

Food systems shape metabolic health through chemical exposures and meal structure, but these layers are rarely connected to continuous glucose monitoring (CGM) phenotypes. We integrated NHANES 2013-2018 biomonitoring, CGMacros real-world meal responses and JAEB-derived CGM trials to define a multi-timescale phenotype of metabolic vulnerability. In NHANES, a 10 percentage-point higher oxidative DEHP fraction was associated with MSI-core (beta 0.168). In CGMacros, adding MSI and food matrix improved subject-held-out high-iAUC discrimination from 0.573 to 0.677, and meal redesign suggested lower high-iAUC risk. In JAEB/Loop/PSO datasets, a locked CGM phase-map was stable (ARI median 0.826) and transported externally for HbA1c/TIR response ranking. These results support triangulated evidence for a food-system-to-CGM digital phenotype and define the bridge cohort needed for same-person validation.
